A drawing and clay modelling competition was organized for the children, and 5 winners were awarded with stationery items and goodies. Every participating child received prizes, snacks, and heartfelt applause, making the experience truly memorable.

Beyond the festivities, La Fiesta carried a deeper purpose — to boost the confidence of differently-abled children, spread compassion, and promote the true power of inclusion. By creating a space where every individual felt valued, La Fiesta helped break societal stereotypes and brought people together in a meaningful way.

At its heart, it was a beautiful celebration of differences, reminding everyone that every child has something truly special to offer the world.

### Impact

### About the Event

On 1st September 2025, Youth United held its highly anticipated Orientation Program to welcome new members and introduce them to the vibrant world of the society. With an impressive footfall of around 150 participants, the event was carefully designed to be interactive, informative, and inclusive — giving new members a genuine look into the spirit and structure of Youth United.

### Program Flow

**Warm Welcome**
The session opened with a warm welcome and an engaging conversation with freshers, helping them feel at ease and comfortable in a new environment.

**Icebreaker — "Question to a Question"**
This improvisational challenge required participants to respond only in the form of questions — no statements allowed. The game lasted 20–30 minutes and sparked laughter, quick thinking, and high energy, creating a lively and inclusive atmosphere from the very beginning.

**Society Introduction**
Following the icebreaker, the focus shifted to introducing Youth United's vision, structure, and initiatives. Freshers gained insights into the various campaigns, projects, and events the society has spearheaded, establishing a clear understanding of the society's purpose and real-world impact.

**Menti Quiz**
The energy was reignited with a Menti Quiz — a fun, interactive session designed to engage freshers further and encourage light-hearted competition among the audience of 150.

**Departmental Overview**
Members took the opportunity to formally introduce Youth United's various departments, providing freshers with a roadmap of opportunities for involvement based on their interests and strengths.

**Executive Board Introduction**
The Team Members and Executive Board (EB) were formally introduced, allowing freshers to put faces to the names leading the society and understand the roles each member plays.

**Music Jamming Session**
The orientation concluded with a music jamming session, adding a soulful, relaxed touch to the closing moments. The session provided a space for self-expression, talent showcase, and bonding — perfectly aligned with the inclusive ethos of Youth United.

### Impact & Outcome

- Facilitated a smooth and welcoming transition for new members into the Youth United community
- The icebreaker activity and quiz stimulated cognitive agility, teamwork, and inclusivity
- Freshers gained a clear and comprehensive understanding of Youth United's mission, enabling them to identify areas for meaningful involvement
- Informal networking during breaks and the music session helped build interpersonal connections between freshers, existing members, and the EB
- These connections are expected to strengthen communal ties and promote sustained engagement throughout the academic year

### About the Event

The BRD Air Force Cycle Rally was organized by DOSA in collaboration with Youth United Society, Paryavaran Welfare Society, and NCC at Thapar Institute of Engineering & Technology. The event was a vibrant celebration of recycling, sustainable transport, and environmental cleanliness.

The rally's central message was powerful in its simplicity — cyclists travelled all the way from **Chandigarh to Patiala entirely on bicycles**, embodying the spirit of eco-friendly and sustainable mobility.

### Event Timeline

**Arrival at Thapar University (10:50 AM)**
Cyclists arrived on campus, where volunteers from Youth United and Paryavaran Welfare Society warmly welcomed them with banners and greetings. A brief interaction session allowed members to introduce themselves, appreciate the cyclists' remarkable effort, and share their common passion for environmental protection.

**Refreshments & Conversations**
Participants enjoyed refreshments on campus, which sparked informal but meaningful conversations about sustainable habits, personal experiences with eco-friendly living, and the broader importance of environmental responsibility.

**Visit to the Thapar Recycling Plant**
Everyone — cyclists, volunteers, and organizers — rode together to the university's Recycling Plant. This served as the central learning experience of the rally, offering practical, first-hand insights into waste segregation processes, recycling operations, and concrete ways to reduce ecological impact.

**Documentation & Outreach**
Photographs were taken throughout to capture meaningful moments and help spread awareness across various social media platforms.

### Impact & Outcome

- Cyclists demonstrated a powerful message about sustainable modes of transport, inspiring students to consider cycling as a viable eco-friendly alternative in daily life
- Enhanced awareness of recycling practices through the Thapar Recycling Plant visit, encouraging students to practice waste segregation and reduce single-use plastics
- The event's social media outreach extended its impact beyond those physically present, sparking wider discussions on environmental responsibility among the campus community
- Strengthened the partnership between DOSA, Youth United Society, NCC, and Paryavaran Welfare Society, paving the way for future sustainability-focused collaborations
- Participants left more conscious of their ecological footprint and more committed to making environmentally responsible choices in daily life

# 5. Utsav-e-Solace
### *A Donation Drive — A Celebration Carried by Hearts*

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Dates** | 27th October – 9th November, 2025 |
| **Category** | Social Impact & Community Service |
| **Semester** | Odd Semester, Academic Year 2025–26 |
| **Organizing Team** | 90–100 members |
| **Total Engagement** | 400+ individuals |
| **Collaborating Agency** | Rotaract Club of TIET |
| **Wall of Kindness Team** | 30 members, 250 engaged |

---

### About the Event

From 27th October to 9th November 2025, the Youth United – Patiala Chapter, in collaboration with the Rotaract Club of TIET, carried out **Utsav-e-Solace** — a two-week donation drive campaign rooted in compassion and community action. The initiative embraced the widely celebrated concept of a **"Wall of Kindness"**, transforming it into a visible symbol of generosity on campus.

### The Wall of Kindness — Collection Phase (4th–7th November)

Between **5:30 PM and 8:00 PM** daily, volunteers set up a desk where residents could learn about the initiative and drop off donated items. On alternate preceding days, fun games were also organized for students on campus to build engagement and awareness. Collection points were set up at hostels and faculty areas across campus.

Students and faculty were invited to donate gently used **clothes, shoes, and toys**, depositing them at the Wall of Kindness installation. A few dedicated Youth United and Rotaract Club members also personally visited faculty areas to collect donations.

The goal was twofold:
1. Mobilise the campus community for a meaningful cause
2. Create a visible, inspiring presence of generosity within the institution

### Distribution Day — 9th November, 2025

On the final day, teams ventured off campus to the heart of underserved neighbourhoods across Patiala:

**Sai Bridh Ashram (Old Age Home)**
Elderly residents received warm garments, bringing comfort and a sense of being remembered.

**Slum Areas near the Old Bus Stand**
Families in need received usable clothing, shoes, and household essentials.

**Masti Ki Pathshala (Children's Activity Zone)**
Children at this community learning space received toys, shoes, and supplies — along with games and interactive activities that added a festive tone to the distribution.

**Tractor Basti**
Families were supported with usable items collected from the campus community.

### Impact & Outcome

- Raised awareness among youth about local inequalities and the power of small, everyday acts of kindness
- The Wall of Kindness created a visible invitation to donate, emphasising reuse and dignity of giving
- Student and faculty volunteers gained direct service experience — organising collections, interacting with beneficiaries, and facilitating distribution
- The initiative cultivated a culture of giving, established meaningful links between youth-led organisations and marginalised communities
- Created a replicable model for future donation drives
- Material donations were transformed into genuine human connection through personal interaction with beneficiaries

> *"Giving need not be complicated — a wall, a drop-off box, a smiling volunteer, a toy in a child's hands — all combine to make solace real."*

# 6. Unity March — Sardar@150
### *Let Us Walk for Unity, Rise for Nation*

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Date** | 17th November, 2025 |
| **Route** | Quila Mubarak Chowk → Kali Mata Mandir, Patiala |
| **Category** | Unity & Civic Engagement |
| **Semester** | Odd Semester, Academic Year 2025–26 |
| **Participants** | 108 |
| **Supported By** | Youth United volunteers |
| **Organized By** | MYBharat, Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports |

---

### About the Event

The Unity March – Sardar@150, supported by volunteers from Youth United, was held on 17th November 2025 to commemorate the **150th birth anniversary of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel** and to promote the message of unity, harmony, and social responsibility among the youth of India.

The event was organized at the district level by MYBharat (Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports), in partnership with Thapar Institute and other colleges and schools in Patiala.

### Event Timeline

**9:30 AM — Assembly**
All participating Youth United volunteers assembled outside the Main Auditorium at Thapar Institute, ready to represent the college with solidarity and purpose.

**10:15 AM — Departure**
Participants boarded buses arranged by the organizers and proceeded to Quila Mubarak Chowk, the official starting point of the Unity March.

**Cultural Program at Quila Mubarak Chowk**
Before the march commenced, a short but vibrant cultural program took place:

- An introductory segment on the purpose of the Unity March and the legacy of Sardar Patel as the *"Iron Man of India"*
- A compelling **Nukkad Natak** by Modi College, spreading awareness on social unity and collective responsibility
- An impactful dramatic performance by the **Tamasha Art Group**
- A graceful **solo dance** performance
- An energetic **Gidda performance** by students of Vir Hakikat Rai School, filling the air with Punjabi cultural spirit

**The March**
The Unity March officially commenced from Quila Mubarak Chowk and covered a route of approximately 40–45 minutes, concluding at Kali Mata Mandir. Throughout the march, participants carried banners promoting unity, peace, and national integrity.

The atmosphere was filled with enthusiasm, discipline, and a shared sense of purpose — making the march not just a walk, but a symbolic act of collective solidarity.

**Langar at Kali Mata Mandir**
Upon completing the march, all participants were served langar at Kali Mata Mandir — a moment of rest, reflection, and community bonding that brought the event to a warm and meaningful close.

### Impact & Outcome

- Created a strong sense of togetherness among volunteers and participants from multiple institutions
- Helped students understand the significance of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel's role in unifying the nation
- Cultural performances enriched the experience by spreading social messages creatively and effectively
- Participants reported increased awareness about social unity, cultural diversity, and civic duty
- Strengthened teamwork, leadership, and organisational skills among student volunteers
- Successfully achieved the objective of promoting unity and left a lasting positive impact on all involved

The event was a celebration of self-reliance, youth empowerment, and national pride — values that Swami Vivekanand championed throughout his life and that remain profoundly relevant for young India today.

### Pre-Event Outreach

A **PR desk** was set up at the COS (Centre of Students) prior to the event, where Youth United volunteers actively interacted with students, provided information about the run, distributed event details, and encouraged participation — contributing significantly to awareness and engagement.

Dr. Robin Singhla (Assistant Professor) delivered an insightful talk on the importance of National Youth Day and elaborated on the life, philosophy, and vision of Swami Vivekanand — inspiring students to embrace self-reliance, discipline, and national pride.

**6:20 PM — Flag Off**
The Swadeshi Sankalp Run was officially flagged off. The run route:
**Library Chowk → COS → Library Chowk → TAN**

Students, volunteers, NCC cadets, and faculty participated with great enthusiasm. Upon completion, **refreshments and gifts** were distributed to all participants and volunteers.

## About Youth United — Patiala Chapter

**Youth United – Thapar Chapter** is a student-driven social service society at Thapar Institute of Engineering & Technology, Patiala. Operating under the banner of **Thapar NGO Society**, Youth United is dedicated to creating impact through initiatives that touch lives, build communities, and bring about meaningful change.

The society works across four core pillars:
- **Inclusion** — celebrating differently-abled individuals and marginalized communities
- **Environment** — promoting sustainability, recycling, and eco-conscious living
- **Education** — supporting underprivileged children through outreach and community learning
- **Community Service** — organizing donation drives, awareness campaigns, and civic initiatives

For more information, visit [youth.thapar.edu](https://youth.thapar.edu) or reach out via the official email at **yu@thapar.edu**.
